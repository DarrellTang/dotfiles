# Voice Rules - Weekly Content Brief

Extracted from Darrell's Voice Guide. Apply to every hook and talking point.

## The Voice

Senior SRE talking to someone earlier in their career. Not explaining AT them - talking TO them.
Peer-to-peer, not expert-from-above. Spoken, not written.

## Hook Rules

REQUIRED:
- Starts mid-story or with a direct question - never with "hey guys" or "in this video"
- Uses contractions: it's, you'll, I've, don't
- References something real: a tool, a number, a situation - not an abstraction
- 1-2 sentences max
- Creates a "wait, what?" reaction or makes the viewer feel seen

BANNED openers:
- "Today I want to talk about..."
- "In this video I'm going to show you..."
- "Have you ever wondered..." (unless genuinely conversational)
- "Let's dive into..."
- Any sentence that could appear in a LinkedIn post

## Tone Examples

BAD:  "Leveraging AI tooling to streamline your DevOps workflow is a game changer."
GOOD: "I spent 3 hours last week fighting a Terraform state lock. Here's what I actually did."

BAD:  "In today's video, we'll explore the benefits of infrastructure as code."
GOOD: "My client asked me why their deploy keeps breaking in prod but not in dev. I knew exactly why."

BAD:  "This tool will transform how you approach CI/CD."
GOOD: "I automated 77 repos in one afternoon. The thing that made it possible was embarrassingly simple."

## Banned Words

game changer, dive in, seamlessly, robust, leveraging, transformative, comprehensive,
delve, groundbreaking, revolutionize, streamline (buzzword use), synergy, ecosystem (overused)

## Dual Audience Rule

Every angle must land for BOTH:
- Developers (comfortable with code, gaps on infra/Linux)
- Ops/sysadmins (comfortable with Linux/servers, gaps on Git/automation)

"You've seen this on job postings" = universal
"You've been frustrated by merge conflicts" = dev-only, avoid

## Talking Points Format

Each talking point should be one concrete thing, not a category.
Something Darrell actually knows from doing it, not general advice.

BAD:  "Discuss the importance of version control"
GOOD: "The specific moment a client's deploy broke because two people edited the same Terraform file"
