# High-Intent Post Patterns

Use these patterns to filter Reddit posts for pain signals worth harvesting. A post needs to match at least one Include pattern and zero Exclude patterns to pass.

The goal is quality over quantity: 15-40 posts from ~250 raw inputs. These patterns catch the posts where someone is genuinely confused, frustrated, or at a decision point — the emotional moments that make great content.

---

## Include Patterns

Match any one of these categories = candidate for the pain bank.

### Direct Confusion
The person doesn't understand something and is asking for help.

- "how do I", "how to", "what is the best way"
- "help me understand", "can someone explain", "ELI5"
- "stuck on", "struggling with", "can't figure out"
- "what am I missing", "what am I doing wrong"
- "why does", "why isn't", "why won't"
- Questions with "?" in the title that aren't rhetorical

### Rant / Frustration
The person is venting about something painful. These are goldmines for "Problems/Negatives" content because the emotional language reveals what tutorials never warn you about.

- "frustrated", "burned out", "burnout", "hate"
- "why is this so hard", "tired of", "nobody told me"
- "I give up", "rant", "vent", "done with"
- "nightmare", "disaster", "hell", "horrible"
- Posts with excessive punctuation (3+ exclamation marks, 3+ question marks)
- All-caps words expressing frustration

### Revelation
The person just had a breakthrough. These reveal what was hard BEFORE the breakthrough — the struggle is the content seed, not the solution.

- "I finally", "after X months/years"
- "it clicked when", "the thing nobody tells you"
- "TIL", "just realized", "turns out"
- "wish I knew", "wish someone had told me"
- "game changer" (when genuine, not promotional)
- "PSA:", "Pro tip:" (when sharing hard-won knowledge)

### Decision Anxiety
The person is stuck choosing between options. These map directly to "Versus/Comparisons" content.

- "is it worth", "should I", "which is better"
- "vs", "versus", "or should I", "considering"
- "switching from", "thinking about moving to"
- "worth the time", "worth the money", "worth the effort"
- "compared to", "difference between"

### Career Direction
The person is trying to figure out their path. High emotional stakes = high content value.

- "what should I learn", "roadmap", "career path"
- "how to break into", "pivot to", "transition from"
- "entry level", "junior", "first DevOps role"
- "career change", "leaving [role] for DevOps"
- "too late to", "too old to", "background in"
- "no degree", "no experience", "self-taught"

---

## Exclude Patterns

Match any one of these = discard the post, regardless of include matches.

### Spam / Promotion
- Body text under 50 words (link-only posts with no real discussion)
- Contains "check out my", "I made a tool", "launching today", "just released"
- Title is purely a URL
- Author is [deleted] with empty body
- Contains affiliate links or discount codes

### Job Postings
- "[HIRING]" or "[FOR HIRE]" tags in title
- "hiring", "we're looking for", "job posting", "open position"
- "apply here", "DM for details", "send resume"

### Meta / Moderation
- "weekly thread", "monthly discussion", "megathread"
- Author contains "AutoModerator" or "bot" suffix
- "rule reminder", "subreddit update", "mod announcement"
- Pinned/stickied threads that are recurring

### Off-Topic for ICP
- Enterprise architecture discussions (audience is mid-level, not principal engineers)
- Vendor-specific sales pitches disguised as posts
- Memes or joke posts with no substantive discussion
